    Lijiang Gule

    Lijiang Ancient music, commonly known as Naxi ancient music, is the ancient music remains of Lijiang Dayan ancient city and its surrounding Naxi settlements. It is composed of three parts: Bongshi Xili (Chinese translation: Baisha Xile), Lijiang Cave music and ritual music. Its music is mainly composed of instrumental music ensembles, and there are also songs and dance songs. Playing the broken stone fine miles of Musical Instruments are tartar pipe, Bobo, Sugudu, Erhuang, Huqin and so on.

    Dongba Ceremony

    Dongba religion of Naxi nationality is a transitional religion from primitive religion of Naxi nationality to artificial religion. As many as 30 kinds of Dongba rituals are the main carrier of Naxi Dongba culture, that is, the contents recorded in the Dongba scriptures with pictoglyphs are expressed through various religious rituals and passed down by religious rituals. These rituals try to explain the contradiction between man and nature and man and society, which are closely related to the production and life of the Naxi ancestors and contain rich cultural connotations. Among them, Sacrifices to Heaven, Sacrifices to wind, Sacrifices to Department and Sacrifices to Ding Bashuo are relatively representative rituals.

    Mural of Lijiang

    Lijiang murals, also known as Baisha murals, are the crystallization of the economic prosperity of the lords in the Ming Dynasty, the absorption of other ethnic cultures, and the development of their own ethnic culture. These murals are distributed in more than ten temples in Baisha, Dayan town, Shuhe, Zhonghai, Yangxi, Xuesong and other villages and towns. They were completed from the Ming Dynasty to the Qianlong period of the Qing Dynasty. The murals are integrated with Han, Tibetan and Naxi cultures, and show the life stories of Tibetan Buddhism, Confucianism and Taoism. Baisha murals are unique in embracing all kinds of religious culture and art schools.
