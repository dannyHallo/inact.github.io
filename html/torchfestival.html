    • "Original Yunnan"-"Torch Festival" of Yi Nationality

    Torch Festival, also known as Star Hui Festival in some ethnic groups, is an important traditional festival for Yunnan ethnic minorities. Yunnan's Yi, Bai, Naxi, Hani, Lisu, Lahu, Pumi and other ethnic minorities have a common festival - Torch Festival. The Torch Festival of Yi nationality has the largest number of people, the largest scale and the highest popularity. It is also the most solemn festival of the Yi people. The festival is generally on June 25 of the lunar calendar, and there are also festivals in June 24.

    The Torch Festival usually lasts three days and three nights. The first day is "Duzai", which means to meet the fire. On this day, every village and village will slaughter cattle, sheep and pigs, greet the God of fire with wine and meat, and offer sacrifices to their ancestors. Women will also rush to make wheat buns and zanba noodles. People outside will go home for a reunion dinner, drink homemade wine and eat tuo meat around the fireplace, and share joy and happiness together. When night falls, the people of the neighboring villages will set up an altar at a spot chosen by the old man, and the holy fire will be lit by striking flint in the traditional way, and the Bhima (priest) will recite the holy fire. Then, every family, by the family old man from the fire pot contact with the torches made of artemisium-sticks, let the children and grandchildren from the hands of the old man to take the torches, first look at every corner of the house, and then the corner of the field, all over the mountains, with fire to drive away the disease disaster. Finally gather on the hillside, play with torches, sing and dance, do all kinds of games. The second day of the torch Festival is "Duge", which means to praise the fire and praise the fire. It is the climax of the torch Festival. At dawn, men and women, old and young, all put on their holiday costumes, with cooked tuo tuo meat and buckwheat buns, and gathered at the altar under the sacred fire. The girls, dressed in beautiful clothes, danced "Dorojo." On this day, the most important activity is the beauty pageant of the Yi family. People dance and sing around the bonfire until late at night, the scene is grand and happy, so they enjoy the reputation of "Oriental Carnival". When the bonfire to go out, a pair of young men and women quietly into the hillside, into the trees, under the yellow oil umbrella, plucking the moon harp, playing the mouthstrings, mutual love. Therefore, some people call the Torch Festival of the Yi nationality the "Valentine's Day of the East". On the third day of the Torch Festival, the Yi language is called "Duha" or "Dusha", which means sending fire. This is the end of the torch festival of the Yi people. When the night fell, after the fire God had finished the dinner, each family lit the torches one after another, held the torches, went to the agreed place, gathered together, set up the fire altar, held the fire ceremony, prayed for the fire god, prayed for the ancestors and bodhissaries, gave the children health and happiness, gave the human harvest and joy.

    When the Yi people celebrate the Torch Festival, at night, the torches are lit in each village, and the torches are scattered and swimming in the fields and villages, which is quite spectacular. The Yi people used torches to illuminate the fields, accounting for the harvest. Folklore, the torch festival is to lead the grain out to see the torch, play the torch is to extinguish the pests and diseases of the seedlings. During the festival, some places kill pigs and cattle to sacrifice to gods; In some places, each family will carry a chicken to the field to sacrifice to the "field common mother". Folk believe that the torch can drive away ghosts, so after lighting the torch to go from house to house, while walking to the torch on the spread of rosin, that is "send". During the torch Festival, there are also various social and cultural entertainment activities, such as singing, dancing, horse racing, bullfighting, wrestling, archery, tug-of-war, swing and so on. The south of the road in the area of the mountain bullfighting, wrestling is particularly famous. Before the bullfight, each village will elect a representative to participate, and the selected fat and strong cattle will be brought to the venue, and the crowd dressed in festival costumes come to watch.
