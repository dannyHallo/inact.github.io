    Torch Festival

    Torch Festival, also known as Star Hui Festival in some ethnic groups, is an important traditional festival for Yunnan ethnic minorities. Yunnan's Yi, Bai, Naxi, Hani, Lisu, Lahu, Pumi and other ethnic minorities have a common festival - Torch Festival. The Torch Festival of Yi nationality has the largest number of people, the largest scale and the highest popularity. It is also the most solemn festival of the Yi people. The festival is generally on June 25 of the lunar calendar, and there are also festivals in June 24.

    The Torch Festival usually lasts three days and three nights. The first day is "Duzai", which means to meet the fire. On this day, every village and village will slaughter cattle, sheep and pigs, greet the God of fire with wine and meat, and offer sacrifices to their ancestors. Women will also rush to make wheat buns and zanba noodles. People outside will go home for a reunion dinner, drink homemade wine and eat tuo meat around the fireplace, and share joy and happiness together.

    Finally gather on the hillside, play with torches, sing and dance, do all kinds of games. The second day of the torch Festival is "Duge", which means to praise the fire and praise the fire. It is the climax of the torch Festival. At dawn, men and women, old and young, all put on their holiday costumes, with cooked tuo tuo meat and buckwheat buns, and gathered at the altar under the sacred fire. The girls, dressed in beautiful clothes, danced "Dorojo." On this day, the most important activity is the beauty pageant of the Yi family.
