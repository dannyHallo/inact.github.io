    • Black Dragon Pool

    Black Dragon Pool is a famous pond in the scenic Jade Spring Park (Yu Quan Gong Yuan) located at the foot of Elephant Hill,
    a short walk north of the Old Town of Lijiang in Yunnan province, China. It was built in 1737 during the Qing dynasty and offers views of the region's tallest mountain, Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, over its white marble bridge.
    In the past, the pool itself has sometimes been dry, spoiling the famous view. In 2010, however, the park was declared a water conservation area by the local government.
    As of 2014, the pool stands full of water, its former beauty restored. The park features several smaller temples and pavilions.

    Must visit:

    Deyue Tower

    Deyue Tower is a pavilion-style building with triple eaves and pointed roofs. The base is octagonal, with three rooms in width and depth. The second and third floors are built with Ruyi bucket arches, and the four corners of the first floor are supported by eaves pillars and corner beams, and the corners are raised and winged. The paintings are colorful and the carvings are vivid.

    Suocui Bridge

    Suocui Bridge is a corridor-style wind and rain bridge built in the Jiaqing period of the Qing Dynasty. The eaves are raised and the green liquid is flying. There are ancient plaques of "Yangqing" and "Suocui" at the bridge head. Shake the tree and see the snow, before the rain falls on the crooked waves."
