    • Mufu Palace

    Mufu is located at No. 49, Guangyuan Lane, Guanyi Street, Dayan Street, Gucheng District, Lijiang City, Yunnan Province, with a land area of 30666.66 square meters.
    The Mufu fully reflects the style and temperament of the Central Plains architecture in the Ming Dynasty, and at the same time retains the simple and rough style of the Central Plains buildings in the Tang and Song Dynasties.
    The Mu Mansion embraces the ancient city. Along the central axis, according to the terrain, there are Zhongyifang, Yimen, the former meeting hall, Wanjuan Building, Dharma Protector Hall, Guangbi Building, Yuyin Building, Sanqing Hall, Side Hall, attic, stage, crossing street building, and home.
    There are 15 buildings including courtyards, corridors, and palaces, with a total of 162 rooms, large and small.

    Must visit:

    Yuyin Building

    Yuyin Building, a famous building in the back garden of the reservoir, was built by Tusi Muchu at the beginning of Yongle in Ming Dynasty. It is the place where the Mu family enshrined the emperor's throne and collected the edicts and inscriptions issued by the emperor to the Mu family from the early Ming Dynasty to the early Qing Dynasty.

    The meeting hall

    The meeting hall is the palace of Mu's chieftain discussing politics and the center for the implementation of power. In front of the hall is a huge square, covered with colored stone slabs, spacious, clean and elegant. In the past, it was the place where the generals were inspected before the expedition, and it was also the place where the Mu family received the congratulations from the people.
