    The predecessor of steam pot is pottery hot pot. The predecessor of Steam Pot Chicken is Yanglin Chicken. In the early years, Yunnan poplar forest. In Jianshui and other places, the precious medicinal material Cordyceps sinensis is used to nestle chickens, which is called "Yanglin chicken", and the pottery hot pot for stewing chicken is called "Yanglin pot". Yanglin pot is produced in Jianshui, and Jianshui pottery has a history of more than one thousand years.
    In the Qing Dynasty, Pan Jinhuai, a master potter, sintered red, yellow, purple, blue and white clay into colored pottery. In 1921, a potter named Xiang Fengchun inherited his ancestral craftsmanship and created a steam pot for cooking.
    In this way, "Steam Pot Chicken" replaced "Yanglin Chicken". Steam boilers are now mass-produced by Jianshui Arts and Crafts Pottery Factory in Yunnan Province. People describe Jianshui pottery as "bright as water, bright as a mirror", "body like iron stone, and sound like a chime".

    Cooking methods

    The tableware for steam pot chicken must be tasted in Jianshui earthenware steam pot. Jianshui steam pot has a simple shape and unique structure. It has an oblate belly and a hollow tube in the center. The steam enters the pot along this tube, and after being cooled by the steam pot cover, it turns into water and drips into the pot to become chicken soup. After two or three hours, the meat cake is ready to eat.
